3. 3D scanning and printing

The assignment

The assignment is to use a design software to make something that could not be made subtractively, and 3D print your design with max 4 hour printing and 100g material. We also need to 3D scan something with 3D photogrammetry and document the process and upload our design files

My inspiration

I googled 3D ideas and found these nice candle holders. I'm going to make one similiar to these ones.

Making the candle holder

I started by watching videos on YouTube that showed how to make different 3D designs in Fusion 360. I started by sketching one half of the design and then revolved around 360°. The diameter is 50 mm and the height is 70 mm.

Next I split the body in two parts and worked with the top part. I made an inner circle with the diameter 47 mm and extruded it to the bottom.

The next step is to hide the top part and work with the bottom. I used Shell and made the thickness 1.6 mm and then I added a bottom thata was 2 mm.

I split the body in two part and work with the top to make a pattern.

Next I made a new scetch and I put two parallel lines from the top to bottom of the design. Then I choose the space between the lines and press extrude all the way in both directions.

Choose symmetric and intersect.

Next we choose Pattern - Circular Pattern, choose body and quantity 18.

Lets choose Pattern - Circular Pattern again and choose the one part that is in the other directions than all the others and put in 18 again.

Here is the final design.

Next we put it into Ultimaker Cura. The design is only 30g but it will take 11 hours and 6 minutes to 3D print it which is far to long for this project so I will have to make a new design that is less complex.
